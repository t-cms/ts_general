The Impact of Poverty on Black Americans

Exploring the intergenerational cycle of childhood poverty to adulthood instability.

Brief Definition of Poverty

  • Poverty has far-reaching consequences affecting individuals, communities, and societies.
  • Poverty is a state where people or communities lack the resources to meet a minimum standard of living.
  • This can include a lack of financial resources, basic healthcare and education, clean drinking water, and infrastructure.
  • In the U.S., people of color face poverty at disproportionately higher rates due to systemic barriers, historical inequities, and ongoing discrimination.

Poverty Series

The poverty rate represents the percentage of individuals within a specific age group whose income falls below the federal poverty threshold. My visualization series explores the link between childhood poverty and long-term instability in adulthood. By measuring success across key indicators—career outcomes, educational attainment, financial security, health, and overall wealth—I aim to illustrate how early poverty can shape a person’s future trajectory. Lastly, I am focusing on Black Americans, as they often face greater systemic barriers and economic disparities that can compound the challenges of escaping poverty.
